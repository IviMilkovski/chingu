Hanbok

Hi everyone! So for the next couple of posts I want to talk a bit more about Korean culture. So today I want to talk about their traditional clothes, specifically the hanbok. So hanbok is a traditional Korean dress that is usualy worn for some traditional occasions such as festivals, holidays or celebrations. It's known to be a very colourful dress and usually when people talk about hanboks they mention the Joseon period. Traditional women's hanbok consists of jeogori, a blouse shirt or a jacket, and chima, a wrap-around skirt, which is usually worn full. The ensemble is often called chima jeogori. Men's hanbok consists of jeogori and loose-fitting baji ("trousers").

What's amazing to me is that they still wear their traditional clothes even today in these modern times and that some clothing companies have found ways to modernize it so that people can wear it with their current clothes. You can see a lot of variations of different types of modern hanboks in kpop music videos. But all in all I think that the hanbok is definitely a big part of Korean culture and for me it's for sure one of the thinks I would love to see and try.ğŸ’™â¤

Bukchon Hanok Village

Bukchon Hanok Village is a traditional Korean village located in South Korea's capital Seoul. This place has a very long history and the name, that is Bukchon, translates to "northern village" and the reason for this name is because this neighborhood is located north of two important Seoul landmarks, Cheonggyecheon Stream and Jongno.

In this village you can see hundreds of traditional houses(Hanok) and many beautiful old streets which have a very long history of almost 600 years. A lot of these hanoks are now small shops, cafes, guesthouses, restaurants, culure centars and even tea houses. I think that all of these places are great for people who want to learn and immerse themselves into the traditional Korean culure. I also want to point out that there are still people who live in this neighborhood so it is advised to tourists to be respectful while going throuth the streets.

This is definitely one of the best places to visit is Seoul if zou are a lover of history. Also one more thing that is great is that it's free so you don't need to pay anything to go and visit this beautiful neighborhood. It's open for visitors everyday exept Sunday and if you want to you can get a guided tour and visit all of the interesting streets and shops that way. Either way I think this is garanteed to be a good experience for anyone who wants to see a bit of older Korean culture.
